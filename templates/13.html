Information:

Occurrence of this pest is usually high during the rainy season. The adult beetle feeds on young leaves and skin of young fruits. Infested fruits get spotted and severe scarring of fruit skin leads to underdeveloped fruit of less commercial value.

Symptoms:

  • The adult beetles feed on various weeds, as well as the young unfurled leaves, stems, and roots of banana trees.
  • They also eat on the young fruit, making scars and spots on the skin that deforms it and makes it unmarketable.
  • Most of the scarring occurs at the base of the fruit, reflecting the fact that beetles chose the most sheltered spots for feeding.
  • Scars are mostly oval in shape and can be confused with those of the fruit-scarring bee Melipona amalthea.
  • The damage is made worse by the colonization of the tissues by opportunistic pathogens. The larvae feed on young roots, and tunnel on the older ones to eat their tissues.
  • Occurrence of this pest is usually high during the rainy season.

Treatment:

  • Maintain a thorough control of weeds in the plantations, as this pest thrives on them.
  • Avoid to plant banana trees near drainage canals.
  • Remove infected plant material from the area.
  • Remove pseudostems below ground level and trim the rhizome where larvae mature.
  • Rake the surface of the soil to expose the pupae to predators.

Fertilizer Recommend:

  • In case of severe infestation spraying with Endosulphan (0.04%) or Carbaryl WP (0.1 %) controls the pest population.
