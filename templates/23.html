Information:

Cucumber downy mildew is a fungal disease that can affect cucumber and other cucurbit crops, causing significant yield losses. The disease appears as yellowish or brownish spots on the leaves, which may also become distorted and necrotic. The disease can spread quickly in warm, humid conditions and may require management to prevent significant crop losses.

Symptoms:

  • Yellowish or brownish spots on the leaves
  • Distorted and necrotic leaves
  • White downy growth on the underside of leaves
  • Stunted growth and reduced yield
  • Fruits may become smaller and misshapen

Treatment:

  • Cultural control: Cultural practices can also help manage the disease. These include planting disease-resistant varieties, ensuring good air circulation and reducing humidity, avoiding overhead watering, and applying appropriate fertilizers.
  • Remove affected plant material: If you notice any plants with symptoms, remove and dispose of them immediately to prevent further spread of the disease.
  • Crop rotation: Implementing crop rotation can prevent the buildup of soil-borne pathogens that cause the disease. Avoid planting cucumbers or other cucurbits in the same area for several years.

Fertilizer recommend:

  • Fertilizers can contain a variety of macronutrients and micronutrients. Nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium are the primary macronutrients, and other micronutrients like calcium, magnesium, sulfur, and iron are also important for plant growth.
  • Common active ingredients in fungicides include copper-based compounds, mancozeb, and chlorothalonil. These products work by inhibiting fungal growth or killing the fungi directly.
