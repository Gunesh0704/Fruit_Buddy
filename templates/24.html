Information:

Gummy stem blight is a fungal disease that affects cucurbit crops such as cucumber. It causes wilting, necrosis, and cankers on stems, leaves, and fruits. Symptoms include water-soaked spots on the stem, yellowing and wilting of leaves, and sunken spots on the fruit. The disease can be controlled through measures such as crop rotation, sanitation, fungicide application, and using resistant cultivars.

Symptoms:

  • Water-soaked spots on the stem.
  • Elongated, sunken cankers on the stem.
  • Cankers that exude a gummy sap.
  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves
  • Sunken, water-soaked spots on the fruit

Treatment:

  • Crop rotation: Rotate the cucurbit crops with non-host crops to reduce the pathogen's build-up in the soil.
  • Sanitation: Remove and destroy any infected plant debris, as well as any volunteer cucurbit plants, weeds, and debris that may harbor the pathogen.
  • Use resistant cultivars: Plant cultivars that are resistant to the pathogen.
  • Monitoring: Regularly inspect plants for symptoms of the disease and take appropriate measures as soon as they are detected.
  • Proper irrigation: Avoid overwatering and practice proper irrigation to reduce the disease's spread.

Fertilizer recommend:

  • Fungicide application: Use fungicides to prevent or reduce the severity of the disease. Apply fungicides according to the label instructions and in combination with other management practices.
  • It is important to note that using organic fertilizers, such as compost or well-rotted manure, can improve soil health and reduce the incidence of soil-borne diseases.
