Information:

Guava is a tree that grows in Central and South America. The fruit is commonly eaten fresh or made into beverages, jams, and other foods. Various parts of the plant, including the leaf and the fruit, are used as medicine. People use guava leaf for stomach and intestinal conditions, pain, diabetes, and wound healing. The fruit is used for high blood pressure. But there is no good scientific evidence to support any uses of guava.

Prevention from Disease:

  • Basic requirements Guava is mainly grown in the tropics and will tolerate temperatures between 15 and 45°C (59–113°F). Guava will grow optimally between 23 and 28°C (73–82°F) but established trees can tolerate short periods at -3 to -2°C (27-28°F) although temperatures below 15°C (60°F) can cause the tree to cease producing fruit.
  • Guava is also amenable to a wide range of soils and will grow in both sandy or rocky soils in addition to loams, preferring a pH of 4.5–7 but tolerating alkaline soil to to pH 8.5.
  • Ensure proper growing conditions: Provide adequate sunlight, well-draining soil, and appropriate levels of water and nutrients for your grapes.
  • Control pests: Use appropriate measures to control pests, such as applying insecticides or using physical barriers.
  • Monitor plants regularly: Check your plants regularly for signs of disease or stress, and take action as needed to prevent further spread.
  • Prune properly: Regularly prune infected branches to improve air circulation and reduce the risk of disease.
  • Apply fungicides: Use fungicides as a preventative measure to protect against common Guava diseases.
