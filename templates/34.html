Information:

Guava red rust is a fungal disease caused by the pathogen Puccinia psidii. It is a serious disease that affects guava plants, causing damage to leaves, fruit, and stems. The disease is spread by spores that are carried by the wind and can infect new growth, causing it to wilt and die.

Symptoms:

  • Yellow to orange pustules on the underside of leaves: The pustules are raised, circular or elongated, and are usually yellow to orange in color. They are typically surrounded by a red or purple border.
  • Brownish-red spores: As the disease progresses, the pustules rupture, releasing brownish-red spores that can be seen on the surface of the leaves. The spores can be spread by wind or by contact with infected plant material.
  • Leaf deformation: Infected leaves can become deformed or distorted, and may curl, cup, or twist.
  • Leaf drop: Severe infections can cause defoliation, with infected leaves dropping prematurely from the tree.

Treatment:

  • Plant resistant varieties: Some guava varieties are less susceptible to red rust and can be planted to reduce the risk of infection.
  • Practice good orchard management: Regularly inspect the orchard for signs of infection and remove and destroy any infected plant material. Maintain good sanitation practices to reduce the spread of the disease.
  • Fungicides: Fungicides can be used to control the disease, but they should be used in combination with other preventative measures. It is important to follow the manufacturer's instructions when applying fungicides.
  • Avoid waterlogging: Guava red rust thrives in humid conditions, so it is important to avoid waterlogging and ensure good drainage in the orchard.
  • Prune and thin the canopy: Pruning and thinning the canopy of the guava trees can help to improve air circulation and reduce humidity levels, which can help to prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Improve plant nutrition: Providing adequate nutrients to the guava plants can help to improve their resistance to disease and reduce the severity of the infection. This can be done by using fertilizers or soil amendments.

Fertilizer recommend:

  • Nitrogen fertilizers: Nitrogen is an important nutrient for guava trees and can be supplied in the form of urea, ammonium nitrate, or ammonium sulfate. Nitrogen fertilizers can help to improve plant growth and yield, but should be applied at the appropriate rate to avoid overfertilization.
  • Phosphorus fertilizers: Phosphorus is another important nutrient for guava trees and can be supplied in the form of superphosphate or triple superphosphate. Phosphorus fertilizers can help to promote root growth and improve fruit quality.
  • Potassium fertilizers: Potassium is important for guava trees as it helps to regulate water use and can improve fruit quality. Potassium fertilizers can be supplied in the form of potassium chloride or potassium sulfate.
  • Fungicides: Fungicides can be used to control guava red rust disease. Copper-based fungicides such as copper sulfate can be used to prevent and control the disease, but should be used at the appropriate rate to avoid phytotoxicity.
  • Insecticides: Insecticides can be used to control insect pests that may spread guava red rust disease. Products containing active ingredients such as malathion, carbaryl, or pyrethroids can be effective against common insect pests of guava trees.
