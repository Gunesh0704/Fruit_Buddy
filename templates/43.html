Information:

Sooty mould is a common disease wherever honey dew or sugary substance secreting insects, such as mango hopper, scales, coccids and mealy bugs are found. Disease is severe in old and dense orchards where light intensity is low

Symptoms:

  • Black velvety covering on the leaf surface.
  • sooty mould on the leaf surface. In severe cases the trees turn completely black due to the presence of mould over the entire surface of twigs and leaves.

Treatment:

  • Prune flowering trees during blooming when wounds heal fastest.
  • Remove wilted or dead limbs well below infected areas.
  • Special Fruiting Hormones for maximising fruits in Plants.

Fertilizer recommend:

  • Insecticidal soap or dish soap (e.g. one tablespoon per 5 liters of water) can be sprayed on affected plants. After letting the soap solution settle on the plants, it can be rinsed off, thereby removing the mold.
  • Concrobium Mold Control effectively eliminates and prevents mold with no bleach, ammonia or VOCs. Concrobium works as it dries by crushing the mold spores at the source, leaving behind an invisible antimicrobial barrier to prevent future mold growth.
  • Controlling of insect by spraying systemic insecticides like methyl dematon.
  • After that spray starch solution 1kg Starch/Maida in 5 litres of water.
  • Starch dries and forms flake which are removed along with the fungus.
