Information:

Tomato leaf mold is a common fungal disease that affects tomato plants, particularly in humid environments. The disease is caused by the fungus Passalora fulva, which thrives in warm, moist conditions.

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing of leaves: The leaves of affected tomato plants turn yellow, particularly on the top of the plant.
  • Brown spots: As the disease progresses, brown spots develop on the leaves. These spots can be circular or irregular in shape and may have a yellow halo around them.
  • Fuzzy gray or brown growth: A fuzzy gray or brown growth may develop on the underside of leaves. This growth is caused by the fungal spores that cause the disease.
  • Leaf drop: In severe cases, infected leaves may drop prematurely.

Treatment:

  • Remove infected leaves: As soon as you notice signs of leaf mold, remove infected leaves from the plant to prevent further spread of the disease.
  • Improve air circulation: Improve air circulation around the plants by spacing them adequately and avoiding overcrowding. This will help to reduce humidity levels and prevent the growth of the fungus.
  • Water the soil, not the leaves: Water the soil around the plant rather than watering the leaves. Wet leaves provide a favorable environment for fungal growth.
  • Apply fungicides: There are several fungicides that can be applied to tomato plants to control leaf mold. These should be applied as soon as you notice the first signs of the disease.

Fertilizer recommend:

  • Tomato plants require a balanced fertilizer that contains the three primary macronutrients: nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, as well as secondary nutrients such as calcium, magnesium, and sulfur.
  • When applying fertilizer to tomato plants, it's important to follow the instructions on the package carefully, as over-fertilization can lead to the growth of lush, succulent foliage that is more susceptible to disease
