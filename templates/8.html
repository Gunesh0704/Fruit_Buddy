Information:

Pentalonia nigronervosa (banana aphid) is an aphid in the superfamily Aphidoidea in the order Hemiptera. It is a true bug and sucks sap mainly from Musa species.

Symptoms:

  • Leaves are bunched into a rosette appearance, stunted growth and leaf margins are wavy with upward rolling.
  • The infested plants turn pale with sticky appearance. The leaves curled and crinkled.
  • Do not produce bunches.
  • Vector of bunchy top disease.
  • Seen in colonies on leaf axils and pseudostem

Treatment:

  • Ensure clean cultivation.
  • Use healthy and pest free suckers to check the pest incidence.
  • Destroy diseased plants with rhizome.
  • Remove the dead plants and burn them.
  • Contact your local Agricultural Extension office for other possible solutions in your area.

Fertilizer Recommend:

  • Spray methyl demeton 25 EC 0.05%.
  • Encourage activity of predators like Scymnus, Chilomenes sexmaculatus, Chrysoperla carnea and other coccinellids.
  • Use kerosene, or a mixture of diesel and mineral oil.
  • After 1 week, spray the infected plants with herbicide (glyphosate).
